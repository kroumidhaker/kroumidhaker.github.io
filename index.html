Profile

Evolutionary game theory, stochastic processes, and combinatorics on words.

Dhaker Kroumi is Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals (KFUPM), Dhahran, Saudi Arabia. He earned his PhD in Applied Mathematics from the University of Montreal (2015) under the supervision of Sabin Lessard. His research spans evolutionary game theory, population genetics, coalescent theory, combinatorics on words, and mathematical inequalities, with a focus on the evolution of cooperation in structured populations.

Research

Core areas

01

Evolutionary Game Theory

Evolution of cooperation, fixation probabilities, aspiration dynamics, multi-player games, structured populations, and network reciprocity.

02

Stochastic Processes

Moran models, Wright–Fisher models, coalescent theory, Markov chains, and diffusion approximations in population genetics.

03

Combinatorics on Words

Primitive words, Hamming-compatible distances, products of words, and Levenshtein spheres.

04

Mathematical Inequalities

Sharp estimates and hypergeometric methods for remainders of alternating series.

05

Complex Systems

Adaptive dynamics, phenotype-based cooperation, sensitivity-driven migration, and cyclic models with asymmetric interactions.
